## Purpose
Surface AI-generated design assistance to the planner — site summaries, constraint and
opportunity narratives, feature placement suggestions, phased build strategy,
risk warnings, pitch/brief drafts, and assumption/data-gap detection. Every AI output
is treated as a draft the user must accept, edit, or reject; outputs carry provenance,
confidence level, and an explicit "AI draft" label. Section is "helpful but only when it
explains itself" per Section 0d AI Guardrails — no silent automation, no unlabeled
inference, no overrides of human design decisions.

## Known gotchas
- **Legacy `ai/` folders exist and are NOT this section.** `apps/web/src/features/ai/`
  (contains `ContextBuilder.ts`) and `apps/api/src/routes/ai/index.ts` pre-date the
  sectioned manifest; the scaffold generator refused the `ai` slug, which is why this
  section uses `ai-design-support`. Do not move or rename the legacy folders without
  explicit direction; they may back earlier AI features that are now counted under
  `ai-site-summary` (status `partial`) and `ai-output-rating-feedback` (status
  `partial`).
- **Section 0d AI Guardrails govern every output here.** No AI output may ship without
  data-source attribution, a confidence level, and a clear "AI draft" label. Outputs
  below Medium confidence must trigger a "needs site visit" flag.
- AI drafts are editable and must be persisted separately from canonical design state —
  accepting a suggestion is a deliberate user action, never automatic.
- Feedback / rating data is an input to future model improvement; treat it as
  first-class telemetry, not ephemeral UI state.
