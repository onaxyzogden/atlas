## Purpose
Design rotational grazing and multi-species livestock systems on the land.
Lets planners draw paddocks, assign species and stocking rates, plan
rotation schedules with recovery periods, and evaluate infrastructure
(water points, shelter/shade, fencing, gates, corridors). Layers in risk
and allocation analyses: predator risk, overgrazing / browse pressure,
fertility / manure distribution, livestock-to-land fit by enterprise,
species and human-animal movement conflicts, and guest-safe buffers for
hospitality programs.

## Known gotchas
- Existing `features/livestock/` uses a live-computed rotation schedule
  rather than a persisted schedule table. P2 "rotation schedule view"
  items read from `computeRotationSchedule()` — do not add a new
  rotation-state slice without reconciling with this path.
- Stocking density is expressed in Animal Units (AU) via
  `computeAnimalUnits()` in `speciesData.ts`. Do not mix head counts and
  AU in new UI without an explicit conversion.
- Guest-safety conflict detection (MT) assumes both a guest zone polygon
  and a livestock paddock polygon exist in the same project — it short-
  circuits silently when either is missing.
- Predator risk is currently a stub analysis with no external data source
  wired in — the P3 item is marked `partial` for that reason.
- MT gating: `guest-safe-livestock-buffer` must remain hidden unless
  `ATLAS_MOONTRANCE` is set. Do not surface it behind only a P-tag check.
