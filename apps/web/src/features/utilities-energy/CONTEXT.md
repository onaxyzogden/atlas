## Purpose
Plan the service layer every regenerative design eventually needs: solar
arrays, battery/power rooms, generators; water tanks, wells, greywater and
septic; rain catchment tie-ins, utility corridors, lighting; firewood,
waste, compost, biochar, and tool/maintenance/laundry stations. P2 items
are click-to-place point placements plus zone notes; P3 items promote the
placements into an off-grid readiness score, redundancy plan, and
energy-demand / dependency mapping. Utility phasing (P2) ties every placed
asset to the Section 15 build order.

## Known gotchas
- Both the scaffolded `features/utilities-energy/` and the live
  `features/utilities/` folder exist. Until the two are reconciled, the
  scaffolded route is a placeholder — do not add real UI here, extend
  `features/utilities/` and update the redirect notice above when the
  live surface moves.
- `useUtilityStore` is persisted under the key `ogden-utilities` with
  `version: 1`. Any schema change to `Utility` or `UtilityType` must bump
  the version and provide a migration, or the user's existing
  click-to-place points will silently drop on rehydrate.
- P3 items (energy demand notes, off-grid readiness composite,
  redundancy planning) are behind `ATLAS_PHASE_MAX >= P3`. The scaffolded
  route is tagged P2; when P3-only handlers land they need a narrower
  `requirePhase('P3')` at the handler level.
- `OffGridReadiness` consumes `sunTrapAreaPct` and `detentionAreaPct`;
  both are nullable. Missing site data must degrade gracefully, not
  throw — the composite is `0` when inputs are absent.
- MapCanvas imports `useUtilityStore` directly for map-layer rendering
  alongside UtilityPanel's placement handling. Coordinate any store
  reshape with the map layer wiring.
