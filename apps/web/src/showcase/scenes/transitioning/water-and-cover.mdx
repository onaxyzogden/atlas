## Water and cover — the soil begins to breathe

Keyline swales slow the water long enough for the land to drink; cover crops keep the soil clothed long enough for the biology to return. On Three Streams Farm these two moves carry the entire Y0 → Y2 transition.

The numbers are measurable. Infiltration adjacent to the new swales climbs from roughly **11.8 mm/hr** at Y0 to **20.6 mm/hr** by the end of Year 1. Earthworm density on the swale-adjacent zones reaches **3.8 per square metre** in the same window, where the eroded baseline carried almost none. Organic matter on the cropped half rises from the 1.4–1.9 % baseline band toward **2.25 %** by Year 2.

The soil begins to breathe. The rest of the arc follows from that.
