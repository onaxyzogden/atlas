## You do not need 180 acres to start

The eight-year arc on Three Streams Farm begins, in practice, with a single decision: one keyline swale cut along a contour, one season of winter rye and crimson clover replacing bare winter fallow, one nursery batch of hawthorn and elderberry whips struck from cuttings. None of these moves require the full parcel. Each one earns the next.

The smallest meaningful first step is the one you can finish well — a hedgerow line you can plant in a weekend, a cover-crop mix you can drill into the field already in front of you, a tributary edge you can fence off and let breathe. The discipline is not scale. It is observation, then action that fits what you observed.

Start where you stand.
