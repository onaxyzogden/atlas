## A vision worth eight years of patience

What if the land you have not yet found could, eight years from now, hold a stable polyculture in which the work of stewardship reads back to you in birdsong and slow water?

By Year 8, Three Streams Farm carries riparian buffers along all three Sixteen Mile Creek tributaries, native hedgerows on every internal fence line, silvopasture knit across the former row-crop half, and a bird-species richness roughly three-and-a-half times the eroded baseline it began with. The continuous-corn signal that defined the parcel in 2024 reads, on the land-cover layer, as a polyculture mosaic.

This is what becomes possible when a piece of ground is observed before it is moved. Begin there.
