## 2. The fiqh question (what the objective must protect against)

A season-pass / membership sold as **prepayment for specific undelivered nights** is the
advance sale of what the operator does not yet possess — *bayʿ mā laysa ʿindak* / gharar —
the same structure that retired the MTC CSRA model (2026-05-04). Per
`feedback_csa_in_catalogues`, it is **surfaced, never silently omitted or reworded**.

The permitted halal form is already written into the codebase verbatim (`marketGarden.ts`
MGD-S1.4 / MGD-S1.6) and the global covenant: **"share entitlement framed as a membership
benefit, not advance purchase"** — entitlement to *access / priority / belonging-benefits*,
not prepayment for a specific undelivered stay. The objective's job is to **surface,
structure-as-membership-benefit, flag, and route to Scholar Council** — it does **not**
itself rule any structure halal (that is the Council's role).

Already-clean baseline (no flag needed): an ordinary **per-stay service reservation** —
deposit + balance on a booked, deliverable stay — which AG-S4.8 c1–c2 already cover.

**Convergence worth noting.** The same answer arrives from three traditions at once. The
fiqh form is "membership benefit, not advance purchase." The CSA movement was *born* out
of Steiner's **associative economics** (Temple-Wilton / Buschberghof, 1980s) — a community
that *associates with* and shares the reality of a farm, explicitly **not** a purchase;
what corrupted CSA into a gharar problem was its drift into "prepay for a veg box." And in
SaaS, **subscription-to-access** (recurring entry to what exists now) is structurally
healthier than **prepaid credits for undelivered units** (a deferred-revenue liability).
The halal structure is therefore also the *authentic* biodynamic one and the better
product — not a compromise.

This also resolves an apparent collision. True biodynamic association meant the community
shared the farm's *risk* (weather, failure), not just its bounty — yet financial
risk-sharing on uncertain future yield is exactly where gharar lives. The membership-benefit
framing keeps the **association** (belonging, participation, presence, stewardship) and
drops the **speculation** (advance purchase of an uncertain harvest): members share the
*life* of the place, not a financial bet on undelivered goods. The two traditions agree
here rather than collide.

**The load-bearing point.** Membership integrity is a *design* problem, not a *labeling*
one. The instrument stays honest only if it carries genuine **non-stay substance**
(community, seasonal / biodynamic events, bounded off-season access, belonging) — otherwise
a "membership" whose value is really just cheaper nights is a nights-purchase in disguise,
substance-over-form. The scopeNote handles the words; the checklist below makes the
substance a requirement.

### 3a. Five new checklist items (proposed verbatim)

> **c7** (`ag-s4-revenue-model-c7`) — "Decide whether a membership or season-pass tier is
> offered at all - default to none unless deliberately adopted; if none, c8-c11 are N/A"

> **c8** (`ag-s4-revenue-model-c8`) — "If adopted, structure it as a membership benefit -
> entitlement to access, priority booking, member rates, and belonging-benefits - with
> each actual stay still transacted as a separate per-stay service reservation (deposit +
> balance on a booked, deliverable stay) and the membership cancellable with pro-rata
> refund of unused access; the membership fee buys belonging and access terms, NOT a
> bundle of specific undelivered nights (avoids bay` ma laysa `indak / gharar)"

> **c9** (`ag-s4-revenue-model-c9`) — "Ensure member value is demonstrably
> non-stay-predominant - real belonging substance (community, seasonal / biodynamic
> events, bounded off-season access) beyond stay discounts - so the instrument is a
> membership in substance, not a nights-purchase in disguise; any member produce-share is
> treated as delivered-not-prepaid per the market-garden CSA guardrail (MGD-S1.4 /
> MGD-S1.6), not as an advance purchase"

> **c10** (`ag-s4-revenue-model-c10`) — "Bound member access, especially off-season,
> within the AG-S3.7 ecological carrying-capacity ceiling and its seasonal sensitivity
> windows; coordinate with AG-S7.8 so member presence supports off-season resilience
> without exceeding load limits; if member access extends beyond any hardened visitor
> precinct, AG-S3.7 must be in scope - the membership can pull it into scope"

> **c11** (`ag-s4-revenue-model-c11`) — "Route any membership / season-pass instrument to
> Scholar Council review before adoption; surface it explicitly, never as a default or
> recommended model; use no CSRA / salam advance-purchase framing"

(Items are self-conditional: a plain agritourism / retreat with no membership tier answers
c7 "none" and marks c8–c11 N/A — so the extension never over-scopes a non-membership
project.)
